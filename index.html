Univerisity of Calgary

The University of Calgary is a land of opportunities from starting your career, founding a company, learning new skills, or to meet likeminded individuals. The Univeristy of Calgary also known as UofC or UCalgary is a public research univeristy founded in 1966, and located in Calgary, Alberta, Canada. The University offers over 250+ undergraduate programs, 80+ graduate, and tons of Professional and Continuing Education.

History

The Univeristy of Calgary was established on 1996 , however dated back to 1906 , one year after Alberta became a province. In 1906, the Alberta Normal School (which would be later known as the Werklund School of Education), began operations, offering learning opportiniues through experianced educators for the province that was eager to grow.

21st Century

During the 21st Century, the Univeristy continued to expanded its horizons, opening numerous buildings such as the Information and Communication Technology (ICT) in 2001, establishing a new branch campus called Univiersty of Calgary-Qatar in 2007, etc.

West Campus Development Project

Created in 2010, the University of Calgary teamed up with City of Calgary in the Development of Lands adjacent to the UCalgary campus, which was formely known as the West Campus Lands. With the Univeristy of Calgary Properties Group Ltd. (UCPG) as the lead developer, they transformed these lands with the aim of generating financial return investment.

Administration

The Univeristy of Calgary follows a shared governance model. Their Shared governance involves an extensive consulation and respect for a diveristy of views, reflecting the Univerisities desire for Equality, Diversion, and Inclusion. This model is composed of a Board of Governors, who oversee managemnet and operation of the university's business and affairs as well as its properties.

The Board of Governors is composed of the chancellor of the univeristy, university president, alumni's nominated by the alumni association, members of the univeristy state, and members of the general public, which includes students, and three associations.
